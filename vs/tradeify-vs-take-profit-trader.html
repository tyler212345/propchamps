Tradeify vs Take Profit Trader

Two firms built for traders who hate the daily loss limit. Tradeify removes it on Select Flex and adds daily payouts; Take Profit Trader removes it on every plan and keeps things simple. Here's how they compare.

Jump to The verdict Full comparison Which to pick FAQ
The bottom line

The short version.

Both are great picks if the daily loss limit is your dealbreaker — they just get there differently. Tradeify removes the DLL on its Select Flex path, adds an optional daily-payout route, runs on one-time payments, and earns the higher reader rating on the site (4.7/5).

Take Profit Trader removes the daily loss limit across every account size — no special plan required — and keeps an exceptionally simple rule ladder. It also brings the deeper payout record of the two ($60M+ vs $5M+, roughly a 2-day median). Tradeify edges it on flexibility and payout cadence; Take Profit Trader edges it on simplicity and track record.

In one line

Want a daily-payout option and path flexibility? Tradeify. Want no DLL on every plan with zero complexity and a longer payout history? Take Profit Trader.

Frequently asked

Tradeify vs Take Profit Trader — quick answers.

Which truly has no daily loss limit? +
Take Profit Trader removed the DLL on all five sizes; Tradeify removes it on the Select Flex path. Both can be traded with no daily loss limit — TPT applies it to every plan; with Tradeify you choose Select Flex. More in the beginner's guide.
Which offers daily payouts? +
Tradeify, via the Select Daily path. Take Profit Trader pays on its standard schedule rather than daily.
Which has the better payout record? +
Take Profit Trader's total is larger — $60M+ at roughly a 2-day median versus Tradeify's $5M+. Tradeify is newer but rates higher with readers. Compare on the payouts tracker.
Are both one-time payments? +
Tradeify moved to one-time payments. Check Take Profit Trader's current checkout for its structure. CHAMP applies at both and is re-verified weekly.
