Take Profit Trader vs My Funded Futures

Two no-daily-loss-limit firms with very different shapes. Take Profit Trader keeps it gloriously simple; My Funded Futures stacks four product paths and the biggest accounts on the site. Here's how they compare.

Jump to The verdict Full comparison Which to pick FAQ
The bottom line

The short version.

Take Profit Trader is the minimalist's pick. The daily loss limit is gone on all five account sizes, the rule ladder is about as simple as prop trading gets, and the payout record is solid ($60M+, roughly a 2-day median). There's one clean evaluation path and a modest CHAMP discount ($40 off).

My Funded Futures is the maximalist's pick: no daily loss limit and no activation fee on every account, four product paths (Rapid daily, Pro, Flex, Builder), the largest ceiling we track ($450K), and discounts up to 50% off — on a bigger total payout record ($80M+, roughly a 6-hour median). Take Profit Trader wins on simplicity; My Funded Futures wins on flexibility, scale, and discount depth.

In one line

Want the simplest possible rulebook and a single clean evaluation? Take Profit Trader. Want product flexibility, no activation fee, and bigger accounts? My Funded Futures.
