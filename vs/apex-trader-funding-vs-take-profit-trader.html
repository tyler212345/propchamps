Apex Trader Funding vs Take Profit Trader

Two of the most-traded futures prop firms — one with the biggest discount on the site, the other with the simplest rulebook and no daily loss limit. Here's how they stack up.

The bottom line

The short version.

Apex Trader Funding wins on price and scale. Code CHAMP unlocks 90% off plus no activation fee — the biggest active discount we track — with a one-day pass and up to 20 funded accounts, backed by $300M+ in payouts at a roughly 4-hour median. The cost of entry is a daily loss limit and a 50% consistency rule on the funded account.

Take Profit Trader counters with simplicity. It removed the daily loss limit across all five account sizes, runs a clean profit-target ladder, and has a strong payout record of its own ($60M+, around a 2-day median). Its CHAMP discount is smaller ($40 off), and it's evaluation-only — no instant-funding path.

In one line

Want the cheapest entry and multi-account scaling? Apex. Want no daily loss limit and the simplest possible rules? Take Profit Trader.

Frequently asked

Apex vs Take Profit Trader — quick answers.

Is Apex Trader Funding cheaper than Take Profit Trader? +
With code CHAMP, Apex has the bigger discount — 90% off plus no activation fee — versus Take Profit Trader's $40 off. Apex is usually cheaper to start; always confirm at checkout. See the live deals page.
Which has no daily loss limit? +
Take Profit Trader removed the daily loss limit across all five account sizes. Apex keeps a DLL. If a no-DLL rule set matters most, Take Profit Trader fits — more in the beginner's guide.
Which prop firm pays out faster? +
Both are fast. Apex runs a roughly 4-hour median on the larger total paid out ($300M+); Take Profit Trader runs a roughly 2-day median on a $60M+ record. Compare the data on the payouts tracker.
Does Take Profit Trader have a one-day pass? +
No — Take Profit Trader is evaluation-only. Apex Trader Funding offers a one-day pass, so it can fund you faster if speed is the priority.
