Apex Trader Funding vs My Funded Futures

The biggest discount on the site versus the most account flexibility. Apex leads on price; My Funded Futures removes the daily loss limit and the activation fee on every account. Here's the breakdown.

The bottom line

The short version.

Apex Trader Funding is the discount leader — CHAMP takes 90% off the evaluation with no activation fee, plus a one-day pass, up to 20 accounts, and the longest payout record we track ($300M+, roughly a 4-hour median). It keeps a daily loss limit and a 50% consistency rule on the funded side.

My Funded Futures is the flexibility leader: no daily loss limit and no activation fee on every account, the largest ceiling we track ($450K), and four product paths — including Rapid with daily payouts. Discounts run up to 50% off and the payout record is strong ($80M+, around a 6-hour median). It can't match Apex's headline 90%-off entry price.

In one line

Want the lowest entry price and a one-day pass? Apex. Want no DLL, no activation fee, and the biggest accounts? My Funded Futures.

Frequently asked

Apex vs My Funded Futures — quick answers.

Which is cheaper to start? +
Apex's headline discount is bigger with CHAMP — 90% off plus no activation fee. My Funded Futures has no activation fee on every account and runs up to 50% off. Apex is usually cheaper to start; confirm at the live deals page.
Which has no daily loss limit? +
My Funded Futures has no daily loss limit on any account. Apex keeps a DLL. If a no-DLL rule set is the priority, My Funded Futures fits — see the beginner's guide.
Which offers bigger accounts? +
My Funded Futures scales higher — up to a $450K ceiling versus Apex's $300K. Both let you run multiple accounts.
Which prop firm pays out faster? +
Both are fast. Apex runs about a 4-hour median on the larger total ($300M+); My Funded Futures runs about a 6-hour median on $80M+ with a daily-payout Rapid path. See the payouts tracker.
