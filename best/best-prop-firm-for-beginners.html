How we ranked

What makes a firm beginner-friendly.

New traders fail evaluations for predictable reasons — a misunderstood trailing drawdown, a surprise daily loss limit, or simply running out of money after a couple of attempts. So we weighted this list toward the things that actually protect a beginner: forgiving rules, a low cost to retry, and a reliable payout record. Account ceilings and headline discounts matter less when you're still learning the ropes — read the full beginner's guide first if you're brand new.

Frequently asked

Beginner prop firm questions.

What makes a prop firm good for beginners? +
The things that matter most are forgiving rules (ideally no daily loss limit, or a clearly explained one), a transparent drawdown method, a low cost to start so retries don't hurt, and a reliable payout record. Flashy account sizes and huge discounts matter less than rules you can actually trade within. Start with the beginner's guide.
Is a daily loss limit good or bad for beginners? +
It cuts both ways — a guardrail that can stop you blowing an account in one tilt session, but also a rule that can end an account on a single bad day. Many newer traders prefer no DLL so they manage their own risk. Take Profit Trader and My Funded Futures remove it entirely.
How much should a beginner spend? +
Start small — a 25K or 50K account is plenty to learn on. Because beginners often need a few attempts, the discount matters: Apex's 90% off plus no activation fee makes it the cheapest way to keep retrying. See the live deals page.
Can a beginner actually pass an evaluation? +
Yes, with discipline. The most common reasons beginners fail are misunderstanding the trailing drawdown and oversizing. Start on a small account, learn exactly how your firm trails its drawdown, and respect the minimum trading days.
