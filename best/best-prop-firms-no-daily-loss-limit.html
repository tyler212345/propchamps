How we ranked

Not all "no DLL" is equal.

Some firms drop the daily loss limit on every account; others only on a specific path you have to choose. We ranked for how completely the DLL is gone, then weighed the payout record and price — because a no-DLL firm that pays slowly isn't a win. Remember: removing the daily loss limit doesn't remove the trailing drawdown, which is still the rule that ends most accounts.

Frequently asked

No daily loss limit, explained.

What is a daily loss limit? +
A daily loss limit (DLL) caps how much you can lose in a single trading day. Hit it and the day — sometimes the whole account — is locked. It's meant as a guardrail against tilt, but it also ends accounts on one bad session. More in the beginner's guide.
Which prop firms have no daily loss limit? +
My Funded Futures (every account) and Take Profit Trader (all five sizes) remove it outright. Tradeify drops it on Select Flex, and Lucid Trading on its Flex and Pro paths.
Is trading without a daily loss limit risky? +
It gives more freedom but shifts all daily risk management onto you — there's no hard stop. It suits disciplined traders who size correctly. You still have to respect the trailing drawdown, which is what actually ends most accounts.
Does no DLL mean no rules? +
No. Removing the daily loss limit doesn't remove the trailing drawdown, consistency rule, or minimum trading days. Those still apply — see the full rule comparison.
